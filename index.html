Why Recycle E-Waste?

Electronic waste contains harmful materials that can damage the environment if not disposed of properly.

Recycling helps conserve valuable resources like gold, silver, and copper.

Proper recycling reduces pollution and supports a sustainable future.

How to Recycle E-Waste

1. Locate a certified e-waste recycling center in your area.

2. Donate working electronics to those in need.

3. Participate in local e-waste collection events.

How E-Waste Affects the Environment

Water Contamination: Toxic substances like lead and mercury can seep into water bodies, contaminating drinking water sources.

Air Pollution: Burning e-waste releases harmful gases including dioxins and furans that can cause respiratory diseases.

Soil Degradation: Hazardous materials from e-waste degrade soil quality, affecting agriculture and plant growth.

Human Health: Exposure to e-waste toxins can cause severe health issues, including neurological damage and cancer.

Biodiversity Loss: Contaminants affect ecosystems, leading to the loss of wildlife and biodiversity.
